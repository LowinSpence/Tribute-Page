William Miller (Preacher)

His failed proclamation of Christ's Second Coming in the 1840s helped estalished the Seventh-day Adventist church in 1863.

William Miller, the Baptist Preacher who initiated Millerism in the mid-19th-century in North America, preaching on the Image in Daniel chapter 2.

Here's a time line of William Miller's life:

  • 1782 - Born in Pittsfield, Massachusetts
  • 1803 - Married Lucy Smith
  • 1810 - Lieutenant in Vermont Militia
  • 1812 - Raised a company of local men and travelled to Burlington, Vermont
  • 1814 - Promoted to Captain
  • 1815 - Returned to Poultney after being discharged from the army. Throughout this period he became deeply concerned with the question of death and afterlife, after his parents died.
  • 1822 - He made a formal statement that the coming of Christ was near - on or before 1843.
  • 1831 - He resigned his Masonic membership and did a public lecture in the town of Dresden.
  • 1832 - Miller submitted a series of sixteen articles to the Vermont Telegraph.
  • 1833 - Miller wrote in a letter to his friends to treat Freemasonary "as they would any other evil".
  • 1834 - Miller published a synopsis of his teachings in a 64-page tract.
  • 1840 - Millerism was established.
  • 1844 - Miller responded publicly in writing about his error acknowledging his disappoitment. However he still believed that the day of the Lord was near.
  • 1849 - Died in Low Hampton, New York.
